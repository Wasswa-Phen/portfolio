FAQ

Common questions from clients

What's your design process for Ugandan clients?

My process begins with thorough research into your target audience and local market conditions. I then create concepts that blend international design standards with Ugandan aesthetic preferences. The process includes multiple review stages to ensure the final product resonates with your audience while meeting business objectives.

How do you handle payments from Ugandan clients?

I accept all major payment methods convenient for Ugandan businesses including mobile money (MTN, Airtel), bank transfers, and PayPal. Payments can be made in UGX, USD, or other major currencies. I typically require a 50% deposit to begin work with the balance due upon completion.

Do you work with clients outside Kampala?

Absolutely! While I'm based in Kampala, I regularly work with clients across Uganda and East Africa. My remote design process is optimized for seamless collaboration, with clients in Nairobi, Kigali, Dar es Salaam, and beyond. Time zone differences have never been an issue.

How do you optimize websites for African internet speeds?

I employ several techniques including image optimization, lazy loading, minimal JavaScript, and CDN usage to ensure fast loading times even on slower connections. All websites I develop are tested on various connection speeds common in East Africa before delivery.

What ongoing support do you offer after project completion?

I offer various support packages including website maintenance, content updates, and design refreshes. Many Ugandan clients choose my monthly retainer option which includes hosting, security updates, and a set number of design/development hours each month.

Do you provide training for my team?

Yes, I offer training sessions to help your team manage and update your website or design assets. Training can cover CMS usage, basic design principles, or content creation best practices tailored to your specific needs and skill levels.
